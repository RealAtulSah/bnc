A Glorious History since 1889

Bihar National College, popularly known as B. N. College, was established in 1889 by two avid educationists and nationalists, Babu Bisheshwar Singh and Shaligram Singh of Kulharia Raj, Bhojpur, Bihar. Following the formation of the Indian National Congress in 1885, these brothers felt the urgent need to stoke nationalist feelings among local students and christened the college "Bihar National College." The colonial British government mounted massive pressure on them to delete the word "National," promising titles of Raja and jagirs in return, but the founders refused to yield.

Due to their resistance, Babu Bisheshwar Singh was expelled from the Calcutta College of Law in his final year, and faced restrictions in practicing law. The colonial administration eventually declared them rebels. Tragically, in 1942, Shaligram Singh's son, Shashi Shekhar Singh, was shot dead during the independence struggle. Jagpati Kumar, one of the famous Seven Martyrs who laid down their lives at the Patna Secretariat, was a student of B.N. College.

In 1892, Calcutta University granted the college the status of a first-level college. In 1923, it was taken under government control as a full deficit grant college. In 1952, B.N. College was formally converted into a constituent unit of Patna University. Located on a beautiful campus on the banks of the sacred Ganges River, the college remains a leading co-educational institution.

The college has produced numerous eminent personalities, including Dr. Moinul Haque (who led the Indian Olympic Team and after whom the Moinul Haque Stadium is named), Lal Narayan Sharma (first Attorney General of India), Prof. Siddheshwar Prasad (Central Minister and Governor of Tripura), and Dr. Bindeshwar Pathak (founder of Sulabh International and Padma Bhushan Awardee). Padma Award winners and Arjuna Award winners have graced B.N. College as teachers or students.
